Alice in Wonderland
Chapter 1: Down the Rabbit-Hole (Modern English Version)

Alice was getting very tired of sitting by her sister on the riverbank. She had nothing to do. She peeked into her sister's book, but it had no pictures or conversations. "What is the use of a book without pictures or conversations?" Alice thought.


The day was hot, and Alice felt very sleepy. She was thinking about making a daisy-chain. She wondered if it was worth the effort to get up and pick the flowers. Suddenly, a White Rabbit with pink eyes ran very close to her.

This was not very strange. It was also not strange to hear the Rabbit say, "Oh dear! Oh dear! I will be late!" (Later, Alice thought this was strange, but at the time it seemed normal.)

But then, the Rabbit took a watch from its vest pocket. It looked at the watch and hurried away. Alice jumped to her feet. She had never seen a rabbit with a vest or a watch before. She was very curious, so she ran across the field after it. She was just in time to see it jump down a large rabbit-hole under a hedge.

Alice followed the Rabbit down the hole immediately. She did not stop to think about how she would get out again.

The rabbit-hole was like a long tunnel, and then it suddenly went down. Alice did not have time to stop. She found herself falling down a very deep well.

The well was very deep, or perhaps she was falling very slowly. She had plenty of time to look around. First, she tried to look down to see what was at the bottom, but it was too dark. Then, she looked at the sides of the well. They were full of cupboards and bookshelves. There were maps and pictures on the walls.

As she fell, she took a jar from one shelf. The label said "ORANGE MARMALADE," but the jar was empty. She was disappointed. She did not want to drop the jar and hurt someone below, so she put it into another cupboard as she fell past it.

"Well," Alice thought. "After a fall like this, falling down the stairs will seem like nothing! Everyone at home will think I am very brave."

Down, down, down. The fall never seemed to end. "I wonder how many miles I have fallen?" she said aloud. "I must be near the center of the earth. That is about four thousand miles down, I think."

She continued to fall. "I wonder if I will fall through the earth! It would be very funny to come out where people walk upside-down."

Down, down, down. There was nothing else to do, so Alice started talking again. "Dinah (her cat) will miss me tonight. I hope they remember her milk. Dinah, my dear, I wish you were here with me! There are no mice in the air, but you could catch a bat. Do cats eat bats?"

She started to get sleepy and repeated the question in a dreamy way: "Do cats eat bats? Do bats eat cats?" She was falling asleep and dreaming about walking with Dinah.

Suddenly, thump! She landed on a pile of sticks and dry leaves. The fall was over.

Alice was not hurt. She jumped to her feet. She looked up, but it was dark. In front of her was another long passage. The White Rabbit was still visible, hurrying down the passage.

Alice ran after him like the wind. She heard him say, "Oh my ears and whiskers, it is getting so late!"

She was close behind him, but when she turned a corner, the Rabbit was gone. She was in a long, low hall. Lamps on the ceiling lit the room. There were doors all around the hall, but they were all locked. Alice tried every door, but she could not open any. She walked sadly and wondered how she would ever get out.

Suddenly, she saw a small, three-legged table made of glass. On the table, there was a tiny golden key. Alice thought it might open one of the doors. But the locks were too big, or the key was too small. It did not open any door.

She walked around the hall again and found a low curtain. Behind it was a little door, only fifteen inches high. She tried the golden key in the lock, and it fitted!

Alice opened the door. It led to a small passage. She knelt down and looked through the passage into a beautiful garden. She wanted to leave the dark hall and walk among the bright flowers, but she could not even fit her head through the door. "My head would be useless without my shoulders," she thought. "I wish I could 'shut up' like a telescope."

She went back to the glass table. She hoped to find another key or a book of rules. This time, she found a little bottle on the table. A paper label on the bottle said "DRINK ME."

Alice was a wise child. "I will look first," she said, "to see if it says 'poison.'" She knew that bad things happen if you forget the rules.

This bottle did not say "poison," so Alice tasted it. It tasted very nice, like cherry pie, custard, and pineapple. She quickly finished the bottle.

"What a curious feeling!" said Alice. "I must be 'shutting up' like a telescope."

And she was. She was now only ten inches high. This was the right size to go through the little door into the garden.

First, she waited a few minutes to see if she would shrink more. She was a little nervous. "I might disappear completely, like a candle," she thought.

When nothing more happened, she decided to go into the garden. But when she got to the door, she remembered she had forgotten the golden key. She went back to the table, but she could not reach the key. She could see it through the glass, but she was too small. She tried to climb the table leg, but it was too slippery.

She was very tired, so the poor little thing sat down and cried.

"Stop crying!" she said to herself sharply. "I advise you to stop this minute!" She often gave herself good advice, but she rarely followed it.

Soon, her eyes saw a little glass box under the table. She opened it and found a very small cake. The words "EAT ME" were written on it with currants.

"Well, I will eat it," said Alice. "If it makes me grow larger, I can get the key. If it makes me grow smaller, I can go under the door. Either way, I will get into the garden."

She ate a little bit of the cake and said, "Which way? Which way?" She was surprised to find that she stayed the same size. Usually, this is normal when you eat cake. But Alice expected strange things to happen now.

So, she finished the cake.
